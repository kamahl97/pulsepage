How to price cleaning jobs as a beginner

The goal is not to be the cheapest cleaner. The goal is to quote enough to cover time, travel, supplies, difficulty, and profit.

Start with time

Estimate how many hours the job will take. Bathrooms, kitchens, pet hair, clutter, and move-out messes add time fast.

Add costs

  • Travel time and gas
  • Supplies
  • Equipment wear
  • Payment fees if any
  • Extra time for communication and scheduling

Use “starting at” when unsure

If you have not seen the home, use a starting price and explain what can increase it. That protects you from underquoting a job that is much bigger than described.

Track every quote

After each job, compare quoted time vs. actual time. That is how your pricing gets sharper.

Cleaning Business Starter Checklist

A simple no-fluff checklist for starting a small cleaning business without guessing every step.

1. Pick the first service

  • Residential standard cleaning
  • Move-out cleaning
  • Deep cleaning
  • Small office cleaning

Beginner tip: start with one clear service before offering everything.

2. Know what to ask before quoting

  • Home size or number of rooms
  • Bathrooms and kitchens
  • Pets?
  • How dirty is it?
  • One-time or recurring?
  • Customer expectations and deadline

3. Build a basic supply list

  • All-purpose cleaner
  • Glass cleaner
  • Disinfectant
  • Microfiber cloths
  • Gloves
  • Trash bags
  • Vacuum/mop access plan

4. Set a starter price rule

Do not quote blind. Estimate time, supplies, travel, and difficulty. Leave room for profit and surprises. If a job feels uncertain, say “starting at” instead of locking yourself into a bad price.

5. Use a simple quote message

“Thanks! Based on what you shared, the cleaning would start at $___ and includes ___. If I arrive and the job is much larger than described, I’ll confirm any price change before starting.”

6. Track every job

  • Customer name
  • Service type
  • Quoted price
  • Actual time spent
  • Supplies used
  • Profit notes
  • Would you take this job again?

7. Upgrade when ready
